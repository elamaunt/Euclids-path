## Where we are

In chapter 07 we squeezed the length of a valid train: even along an infinite affine line the number of genuinely reachable centres is short (`cubic_squeeze`). The `+1` fuel is inexhaustible, but the engine's valid move is cramped.

Now we turn the clamp into the additive plane: instead of the length of a *single* move, we ask how many *distinct* K-fold combinations of divisors can fit into a bounded window before two of them inevitably coincide in sum. The answer тАФ a pure pigeonhole, the boxes principle (see the [glossary](GLOSSARY.md)) тАФ gives us the first structural prohibition on unchecked accumulation of repeats.

## Setting: an additive cycle on K-multisets

Fix a finite set of divisors (or centres) `ЁЭТЬ тКЖ [1, T]`, that is, every `a тИИ ЁЭТЬ` satisfies `1 тЙд a тЙд T`. The parameter `K тЙе 1` is the order of the combination тАФ the "length" of the additive word that the engine assembles from elements of `ЁЭТЬ`.

> **Definition (K-multiset).** A K-multiset over `ЁЭТЬ` is a multiset of cardinality `K` all of whose elements lie in `ЁЭТЬ`. The set of all such objects is written in Lean/Mathlib as `ЁЭТЬ.sym K` (the symmetric K-power). Order in a multiset does not matter and repeats are allowed тАФ this is precisely an "unordered choice of `K` elements from `ЁЭТЬ` with replacement".

To each K-multiset `s тИИ ЁЭТЬ.sym K` we assign its additive weight тАФ the sum of its elements:

$$
\Sigma(s) \;=\; \sum_{x \in s} x \;=\; (s : \mathrm{Multiset}\ \mathbb{N}).\mathrm{sum}.
$$

> **Definition (nontrivial additive cycle).** By a nontrivial additive (Euclidean) cycle we mean a pair of distinct K-multisets `s тЙа t` from `ЁЭТЬ.sym K` with equal weight:
> $$
> s \neq t, \qquad \Sigma(s) \;=\; \Sigma(t).
> $$
> "Nontriviality" here is exactly `s тЙа t`: the trivial equality `╬г(s) = ╬г(s)` costs nothing; what is substantive is that *two different ways* of adding up `K` divisors give one and the same total.

The name "cycle" is not accidental. The equality `╬г(s) = ╬г(t)` with `s тЙа t` is the relation $\sum_{x\in s} x - \sum_{y\in t} y = 0$, that is, a nontrivial integer linear dependence (with coefficients `┬▒1`) among the elements of `ЁЭТЬ`. Such a closed additive loop is precisely a "cycle" in the additive structure generated by the carrier.

## Theorem: mass forces a cycle

The chapter's main result is the contrapositive of Lemma B_K (┬зVI, Lemma 6.1.1). The direct lemma asserts: if `ЁЭТЬ тКЖ [1,T]` has *no* nontrivial equality of K-sums, then the set is forced to be small, `|ЁЭТЬ| \ll_K T^{1/K}`. We, however, need precisely the contrapositive тАФ the form that feeds Fan-Cycle further down the chain: as soon as there is enough combinatorial mass, a cycle is *bound* to exist.

> **Theorem** (`exists_additive_cycle`). Let `ЁЭТЬ : Finset тДХ`, `K T : тДХ`, `1 тЙд K`, and suppose
> $$
> 1 \le a \le T \quad \text{for all } a \in \mathcal{A}, \qquad K \cdot T \;<\; |\,\mathcal{A}.\mathrm{sym}\ K\,|.
> $$
> Then there exist `s, t тИИ ЁЭТЬ.sym K` such that `s тЙа t` and `╬г(s) = ╬г(t)` тАФ a nontrivial additive Euclidean cycle.

The condition `K ┬╖ T < |ЁЭТЬ.sym K|` is the mass threshold: the number of distinct K-multisets exceeds the number `K┬╖T` of possible values of the sum. The whole proof is pigeonhole тАФ no analysis, no distribution of primes, no sieve. This makes the result an atomic law of the engine, not a heuristic.

## Why this is true: walking through the proof

The proof in `BK.lean` is arranged in three steps, and each honestly plays its part.

**Step 1 тАФ the image of the weight is pinned inside the window `[K, K┬╖T]`.** Let `s тИИ ЁЭТЬ.sym K`. By definition `s` is a multiset of cardinality exactly `K` (in Lean this is `s.2 : (s : Multiset тДХ).card = K`), all of whose elements lie in `ЁЭТЬ` and are therefore squeezed between `1` and `T`. Hence two bounds:

$$
\Sigma(s) \;\ge\; K \cdot 1 \;=\; K
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\Sigma(s) \;\le\; K \cdot T.
$$

The lower bound is `Multiset.card_nsmul_le_sum` (a sum of `K` terms, each `тЙе 1`, is at least `K`); the upper is `Multiset.sum_le_card_nsmul` (each term `тЙд T`, hence the sum is `тЙд K┬╖T`). In the code this is packaged as

```lean
have hmaps : тИА s тИИ ЁЭТЬ.sym K, (s : Multiset тДХ).sum тИИ Finset.Icc K (K * T)
```

that is, the weight map `╬г : ЁЭТЬ.sym K тЖТ тДХ` really does land in the integer interval `Finset.Icc K (K*T)`.

**Step 2 тАФ the window of values is tight.** The interval `Finset.Icc K (K*T)` contains exactly `K┬╖T тИТ K + 1` integer points (`Nat.card_Icc`). With `1 тЙд K` this is at most `K┬╖T`. The hypothesis `K┬╖T < |ЁЭТЬ.sym K|` then gives

$$
|\mathrm{Icc}(K,\,K{\cdot}T)| \;=\; K{\cdot}T - K + 1 \;\le\; K{\cdot}T \;<\; |\mathcal{A}.\mathrm{sym}\ K|.
$$

In Lean this is the single arithmetic line, closed by `omega`:

```lean
have hcard_lt : (Finset.Icc K (K * T)).card < (ЁЭТЬ.sym K).card := by
  rw [Nat.card_Icc]; omega
```

There are strictly fewer values of the sum than there are K-multisets.

**Step 3 тАФ pigeonhole.** The map `╬г` carries the larger set (all K-multisets) into the smaller one (the window of sums). By Dirichlet's principle it cannot be injective: two distinct preimages get glued into one image. Formally this is `Finset.exists_ne_map_eq_of_card_lt_of_maps_to`, which yields exactly the pair we need:

```lean
obtain тЯиs, hs, t, ht, hne, heqтЯй :=
  Finset.exists_ne_map_eq_of_card_lt_of_maps_to hcard_lt hmaps
```

`hne : s тЙа t` and `heq : ╬г(s) = ╬г(t)` тАФ this is the nontrivial additive cycle. The proof is complete.

> **Note.** The three steps match the three ingredients of any pigeonhole argument: (1) send the objects into boxes (`hmaps` тАФ the weight lands in the window), (2) count that the boxes are few (`hcard_lt`), (3) extract the collision (`Finset.exists_ne_map_eq_of_card_lt_of_maps_to`). None of the steps uses any property of prime numbers тАФ *any* `ЁЭТЬ тКЖ [1,T]` will do. This is precisely why it is a law of the mechanism, not a property of a particular arithmetic.

## What it means: a bound on the order of repeats

The substantive consequence is a bound on how long the carrier can grow "cycle-free". Let us restate the direct Lemma B_K as a counting estimate.

> **Observation (cycle-freeness threshold).** If `ЁЭТЬ тКЖ [1,T]` contains no nontrivial additive cycle of order `K`, then the weight map `╬г` is injective on `ЁЭТЬ.sym K`, whence
> $$
> |\mathcal{A}.\mathrm{sym}\ K| \;\le\; |\mathrm{Icc}(K,\,K{\cdot}T)| \;=\; K{\cdot}T - K + 1 \;\le\; K{\cdot}T.
> $$
> Since `|ЁЭТЬ.sym K|` grows like $\binom{|\mathcal{A}| + K - 1}{K} \asymp |\mathcal{A}|^K / K!$, from $|\mathcal{A}|^K \lesssim K{\cdot}T$ we get $|\mathcal{A}| \ll_K T^{1/K}$. This is exactly the `|ЁЭТЬ| тЙк_K T^{1/K}` of ┬зVI.

**Conclusion.** An additive cycle can be avoided only by keeping the carrier *radically* sparse тАФ no more than on the order of `T^{1/K}` elements in the window `[1,T]`. Euclid's engine, on the contrary, is forced to accumulate divisors on the centres along its move; as soon as enough of them pile up relative to the available window of values, a repeat of sums is inevitable. Through the contrapositive this translates into a structural prohibition: *the order of repeats is bounded* тАФ one cannot endlessly clone a divisor configuration while keeping all additive weights distinct.

Coupled with the impossibility of the perpetual engine тАФ the infinite strictly descending chain, the programme's central forbidden object (chapter 01, `no_infinite_descent`) тАФ this feeds the prohibition of *perpetual clean recycling*: if the reuse of divisors could go on without forming an additive loop, the carrier would grow while remaining cycle-free, contradicting the `T^{1/K}` threshold. The cycle guaranteed by `exists_additive_cycle` is the point where "clean" fuel recycling closes onto itself and therefore cannot last forever.

> **Note (the boundary of the result).** The theorem gives the *existence* of a cycle, but not its geometry: it does not say *which* divisors enter it, and it does not tie the cycle to differences of centres. That tie тАФ that a repeat of the divisor `тДУ` pins down `тДУ тИг (nтВВ тИТ nтВБ)` тАФ is established only in the next chapter. Here we record honestly: `exists_additive_cycle` is a combinatorial threshold, NOT a structural rigidity; the two must not be conflated.

## Bridge to what comes next

We have obtained that a mass of divisors forces an additive cycle, and, through the contrapositive, that the order of repeats is bounded by the window `T^{1/K}`. But the *bare* existence of coinciding sums does not yet say that a repeated divisor entails periodicity of hits along the move.

In chapter 09 (`factor_repeat_rigidity`, `cross_side_fuel`) we will give the repeat its rigidity: if a prime `тДУ` appears as a divisor at two centres, it divides their difference, `тДУ тИг (nтВВ тИТ nтВБ)`. The bounded cycle of this chapter and the rigidity of the next, together with the short train (chapter 07), will close the covering into something finite and short тАФ turning the additive prohibition into a geometric one.
