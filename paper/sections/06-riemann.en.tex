\section{The Riemann hypothesis through the engine}\label{sec:riemann}

The Riemann hypothesis (RH) lives in a different world from the twins --- complex analysis, the
zeta function, the nontrivial zeros in the critical strip~\cite{clay-rh} --- yet the same engine
reaches it, and by the same argument by contradiction. We are emphatic that RH is \red{} open: the
theorems below are honest reductions and a decree, never a proof. Two independent routes carry the
engine to the zeros --- a contraposition through an analytic bridge, and a translation into the
Liouville sign --- and both run into one wall. A third route derives RH from the first cause,
\yellow{} axiom-tainted, with its price disclosed by machine. Throughout, the goal is the official
Riemann hypothesis of \code{mathlib} (a quantifier over all nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta
function), not a home-made predicate.

\subsection*{Contraposition: not-RH begets an engine}

The logical form is exactly that of the twin branch: for $P := \mathrm{RH}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:riemann-contraposition}
  \bigl(\neg P \Rightarrow \text{Engine}\bigr)\ \wedge\ \neg\,\text{Engine}\ \Longrightarrow\ P .
\end{equation}
Substantively we build $\neg\mathrm{RH} \Rightarrow \exists$ a zero $\rho$ with
$\mathrm{Re}\,\rho \ne \tfrac12 \Rightarrow$ perpetual engine $\Rightarrow \bot$, where the last
step is the machine-checked impossibility of infinite descent and the middle step is the branch's
only open node. We stress that RH does \emph{not} follow from the infinitude of the twins: these are
independent statements, and we never record ``twins $\Rightarrow$ RH''. The branch reuses the proven
mechanism and the contraposition scheme, nothing more.

\begin{definition}[nontrivial zero]\label{def:NontrivialZeroM}
A nontrivial zero is a $\rho$ with
$\zeta(\rho)=0 \wedge (\neg\exists n,\ \rho = -2(n{+}1)) \wedge \rho \ne 1$,
exactly the premise of \code{mathlib}-RH gathered into one object. \leantag{NontrivialZeroM}
\end{definition}

To feed the engine we must localise such a zero into the strip $0<\mathrm{Re}\,\rho<1$. The top edge
$\mathrm{Re}\,\rho<1$ is closed by \code{mathlib} analysis (a PNT-level result proven there and not
by us, \code{riemannZeta\_ne\_zero\_of\_one\_le\_re}); the bottom edge $\mathrm{Re}\,\rho>0$ once
required a separate input on the trivial zeros below the line, later discharged
(\cref{thm:trivialBelowZeroClassification}). Together these place a nontrivial zero strictly inside
the strip.

All substantive difficulty is gathered into one proposition, the \emph{engine bridge}. It is not an
axiom: it is an explicit premise of a conditional theorem, kept honestly open.

\begin{definition}[engine bridge]\label{def:EngineBridge}
The engine bridge asserts that every off-line nontrivial zero yields a perpetual engine:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:EngineBridge}
\begin{aligned}
  &\forall\,\rho,\ \zeta(\rho)=0 \wedge 0<\mathrm{Re}\,\rho \wedge \mathrm{Re}\,\rho<1 \wedge \mathrm{Re}\,\rho \ne \tfrac12\\
  &\qquad\Longrightarrow \exists\,A\ge 1,\ \exists\,H:\N\to\N,\ \forall t,\ \DescentStep A\,(H\,t)\,(H(t{+}1)).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
\leantag{EngineBridge}
\end{definition}

The witness $H$ with $\DescentStep A\,(H\,t)\,(H(t{+}1))$ for all $t$ is exactly an infinite clean
chain of strictly decreasing height --- a perpetual engine. Substantively, balance at $\tfrac12$ is
the point where gain and loss of mass along the descent compensate; a zero off $\tfrac12$ removes
the compensation and yields a free directed pumping, the engine forbidden by the acyclicity of the
descent rank. We do not mask that the engine bridge is unproven and, in any foreseeable form,
comparable in difficulty to RH itself. The branch is honest only because all of its unprovenness is
localised in this one premise.

\begin{theorem}[RH from the engine bridge]\label{thm:riemannHypothesis_of_engine_bridge}
\green{}\ If the trivial zeros are classified and the engine bridge holds, then the \code{mathlib}
Riemann hypothesis follows. \leantag{riemannHypothesis\_of\_engine\_bridge}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof idea]
Take an arbitrary zero $\rho$ nontrivial and $\ne 1$; localise it into the strip. Suppose
$\mathrm{Re}\,\rho \ne \tfrac12$. The bridge yields $A\ge1$ and a chain $H$ --- a perpetual engine
--- contradicting the acyclicity of the descent rank. Hence $\mathrm{Re}\,\rho = \tfrac12$. All
analysis sits in the premise; the tail (engine $\Rightarrow \bot$) is a machine-checked fact shared
with the twins. RH is obtained \emph{conditionally} on the bridge, and not one iota more.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[not-RH begets an engine]\label{thm:not_RH_gives_engine}
\green{}\ If the engine bridge holds and RH fails, then there exist $A\ge 1$ and $H:\N\to\N$ with
$\DescentStep A\,(H\,t)\,(H(t{+}1))$ for all $t$. \leantag{not\_RH\_gives\_engine}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} Unfold $\neg\mathrm{RH}$, extract a witness zero off $\tfrac12$, feed it to the
bridge. The contradiction with the acyclicity of the descent rank is literally the one that closes
the twins --- a shared finale.

\subsection*{The two-transport route: one shared node}

We place the branch next to the twins structurally, showing that its open node is the same carrier
of two already proven in the core, not new analysis.

\begin{definition}[two-transport law]\label{def:TwoTransportLaw}
The two-transport law states that for all $m\ge1$ and all factorisations
$6m-1 = a b v$ and $6m+1 = q r s$ one has $q r s = a b v + 2$ --- the identity ``the right companion
exceeds the left by two'' at the level of factorisations. \leantag{TwoTransportLaw}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[the two-transport law holds]\label{thm:twoTransportLaw_holds}
\green{}\ The two-transport law holds. \leantag{twoTransportLaw\_holds}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} It is not an axiom or a hypothesis but a witnessed proposition: the concrete
rank-$3{\times}3$ determinant identity from the core supplies the witness.

\begin{definition}[two-transport bridge]\label{def:TwoTransportBridge}
The two-transport bridge is the \emph{implication} ``two-transport law $\Rightarrow$ engine bridge''.
It is an implication, not an equivalence of hypotheses: we do not identify RH with the twins, only
ask whether the bridge RH needs follows from the already-proven core. \leantag{TwoTransportBridge}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RH from two-transport]\label{thm:riemannHypothesis_of_two_transport}
\green{}\ If the trivial zeros are classified and the two-transport bridge holds, then the
\code{mathlib} Riemann hypothesis follows. \leantag{riemannHypothesis\_of\_two\_transport}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} Plug the proven two-transport law (\cref{thm:twoTransportLaw_holds}) into the
link to obtain the engine bridge, then apply \cref{thm:riemannHypothesis_of_engine_bridge}. This
names precisely the price of the branches' simultaneity: what stays open is exactly the
two-transport bridge --- one implication ``carrier of two $\Rightarrow$ bridge'' --- and no separate
zeta analysis.

\subsection*{The Liouville route: $\lambda = (-1)^{\mathrm{rank}}$}

Instead of building our own bridge to the zeros, we take a ready-made arithmetic equivalent of RH
and decompose \emph{it}. The Liouville function $\lambda(n) = (-1)^{\Om(n)}$, with $\Om(n)$ the
number of prime factors of $n$ with multiplicity, has summatory function
$L(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{x}\lambda(n)$, the accumulated imbalance between integers with an even and an odd
number of prime factors.

\begin{definition}[Liouville bound]\label{def:LiouvilleBound}
The Liouville bound is the estimate
$\forall\,\varepsilon>0\ \exists\,C>0\ \forall x:\ |L(x)| \le C\,x^{1/2+\varepsilon}$
--- the sum of $\pm1$ grows like a random walk, no faster than $\sqrt{x}$ up to $x^{\varepsilon}$.
\leantag{LiouvilleBound}
\end{definition}

The supporting fact is a classical theorem of analytic number theory that \code{mathlib} does not
yet have; we introduce it as an explicit input-bridge rather than postulating RH.

\begin{definition}[Liouville--RH bridge]\label{def:LiouvilleRHBridge}
The Liouville--RH bridge is the equivalence ``Liouville bound $\iff$ Riemann hypothesis'' --- the
classical equivalence of the $O(x^{1/2+\varepsilon})$ bound with the absence of zeros right of the
critical line. \red{} \leantag{LiouvilleRHBridge}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RH from the Liouville bound]\label{thm:riemann_of_liouville_bound}
\green{}\ The Liouville--RH bridge together with the Liouville bound implies the Riemann hypothesis.
\leantag{riemann\_of\_liouville\_bound}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} A one-line application of the bridge to the bound: all analysis is isolated in the
bridge, and the only substantive goal is the arithmetic Liouville bound.

The reason this fits our apparatus is a coincidence of objects: the number of prime factors is
exactly our rank, and stripping a factor flips the sign.

\begin{theorem}[the Liouville sign is rank parity]\label{thm:liouville_eq_neg_one_pow_rank}
\green{}\ For $n\ne0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:liouville-rank}
  \lambda(n) = (-1)^{\Om(n)} = (-1)^{\mathrm{rank}} .
\end{equation}
Proven with no inputs of ours. \leantag{liouville\_eq\_neg\_one\_pow\_rank}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[a prime flips the sign]\label{thm:liouville_flip_of_mul_prime}
\green{}\ For a prime $p$ and $m\ne0$, $\lambda(p\cdot m) = -\lambda(m)$.
\leantag{liouville\_flip\_of\_mul\_prime}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} $\Om(p\cdot m) = \Om(m)+1$, whence the sign flips. Thus
$L(x) = \sum_{n\le x}(-1)^{\mathrm{rank}(n)}$ is the rank-parity imbalance on $[1,x]$, and the
factor-stripping operator --- the rank-descent step of the twin branch --- is exactly the operator
flipping the Liouville sign. The two apparatuses, invented for different problems, are one operator
on one carrier.

We do not prove the Liouville bound. Its difficulty is the classical \emph{parity wall}: control of
the parity of $\Om$, inaccessible to sieve methods and in substance equivalent to RH itself. Our
descent supplies \emph{vertical} information (one flip per stripped factor), while the bound demands
a \emph{horizontal} statistical balance over all $n\le x$; the passage between the two is the open
node. (The stronger P\'olya claim $L(x)\le0$ is refuted, so the target is the bound, not a fixed
sign.)

\begin{conjecture}[rank balance]\label{conj:rank-balance}
\red{}\ The descent sign dynamics (\cref{thm:liouville_flip_of_mul_prime}) together with the
acyclicity of the descent rank entails a statistical balance of rank parities, i.e.\ the Liouville
bound. Equivalently, the asymmetry $\mathrm{Re}\,\rho \ne \tfrac12$ of a nontrivial zero forces the
violation of two-transport balance that begets an engine.
\end{conjecture}

Both routes run into one wall, ``asymmetry $\Rightarrow$ engine'', dressed differently. In the
common language of rank parity $\mathrm{rank}(n) = \Om(n)$ the twin wall (balance of side ranks,
$N_{00}$ not emptying relative to $N_{33}$) and the Riemann wall (balance of the parity of a single
$n$) are one node --- the marginal a shadow of the joint. This unity is a conjecture about the
\emph{nature} of the difficulty, not a logical derivation of one hypothesis from the other.

\subsection*{Closing an input honestly: the trivial zeros}

One former analytic input is now a theorem, and the load did not move --- it vanished.

\begin{theorem}[the trivial zeros, classified]\label{thm:trivialBelowZeroClassification}
\green{}\ Every zero of $\zeta$ with $\mathrm{Re}\,s\le0$ is trivial:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:trivial-zeros}
  \forall s\in\mathbb{C},\quad \zeta(s)=0 \wedge \mathrm{Re}\,s\le0 \Rightarrow \exists n\in\N,\ s = -2(n{+}1) .
\end{equation}
\leantag{trivialBelowZeroClassification}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} The route is entirely \code{mathlib}: $s=0$ is excluded
($\zeta(0)=-\tfrac12$); for $s\ne0$ the functional equation at $w=1-s$ (with $\mathrm{Re}\,w\ge1$)
makes the nontrivial factors nonzero, so the cosine vanishes: $\cos(\pi(1-s)/2)=0 \Rightarrow s=-2k$,
$k\ge1$. Consequently the strip localisation becomes unconditional and RH is conditional \emph{only}
on the bridge.

Two audit findings keep the rest honest. First, several ``impossible-engine'' reformulations are
provably circular: because the factory is unconditionally impossible, a coherent two-transport
bridge is equivalent to RH \emph{verbatim}.

\begin{theorem}[a coherent bridge is RH verbatim]\label{thm:coherentTwoTransportBridge_iff_RH}
\green{}\ A coherent two-transport bridge is equivalent to the Riemann hypothesis.
\leantag{coherentTwoTransportBridge\_iff\_RH}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} A coherent law would build a factory, and the factory is unconditionally empty;
the decomposition is a map of obligations, its audit gates inert markers. Such routes are exact
reformulations of RH, not reductions of difficulty. Second, an adversarial probe cracked open a
rank-jump ``wrapper'' that held for free (a \warn{} vacuity): the honest remainder shows that, for
any jump-free system, pairing is exactly the absence of a Liouville violation --- again RH strength,
not a reduction. Catching these by machine is why the parts that passed can be trusted.

\subsection*{The manifestation front: a green chain, RH from the decree}

The last route mirrors the twin architecture. A zero off the critical line is an \emph{unpaid
deviation}; RH becomes ``deviations do not exist''. Each such deviation carries a scale, the integer
part of $|\mathrm{Im}\,s|$.

\begin{definition}[unpaid deviation supply]\label{def:DeviationFlowSupply}
An unpaid supply at ledger scale $(A,M_0)$ is an infinite admissible family of extended generated
flows,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:deviation-supply}
  \exists\,\mathcal{F}\subseteq\mathrm{ExtProperFlow}(A,M_0),\ \mathrm{InfiniteFamily}(A,M_0,\mathcal{F}) .
\end{equation}
This is the \emph{same} object the twin boundary conjecture builds; it is delivered by the genuine
rank machine, not an empty abstraction. \leantag{DeviationFlowSupply}
\end{definition}

The \emph{manifestation law} decrees that a deviation gives itself away by such a supply wherever the
books reconcile: for every deviation of scale $Z$ and every $M_0\ge Z$, every resolving projection
carries an unpaid supply. Crucially, the law asserts a \emph{positive event} (zero $\to$
manifestation), not ``there are no zeros'': its strength depends on decreeing a cause, not a
conclusion. The ``cannot'' side is a green theorem.

\begin{theorem}[no unpaid supply at a resolved scale]\label{thm:no_deviationFlowSupply_at_resolved_scale}
\green{}\ For any projection at scale $(A,M_0)$ that resolves collisions, no unpaid deviation supply
exists at $(A,M_0)$. \leantag{no\_deviationFlowSupply\_at\_resolved\_scale}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} A resolving projection yields a stable energy-free universe; an infinite family of
flows collides under the finite key (pigeonhole), the collision assembles a witness engine, and
engines do not exist --- the same three moves that killed the finiteness of the twins. We prove the
``cannot'' side; only ``is obliged to manifest'' is decreed.

\begin{theorem}[RH from manifestation and boundary]\label{thm:riemannHypothesis_of_manifestation_and_boundary}
\green{}\ The absence of engines, the accepted twin boundary, and the manifestation law together
imply the Riemann hypothesis (in the precise \code{mathlib} sense).
\leantag{riemannHypothesis\_of\_manifestation\_and\_boundary}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} The boundary yields a resolving projection at the zero's height, the law turns the
zero into an infinite supply, the supply assembles a witness engine, and it is killed by ``engines do
not exist''; then RH is extracted from ``no deviations'' using
\cref{thm:trivialBelowZeroClassification}. All three premises do real work, not through explosion.
This entire chain is \green{}: it lives in a module that does not import the quarantine.

The axiom enters at exactly one step, when the manifestation law is placed into the first cause as
its second boundary (alongside the twin boundary) --- but the axiom is still one, \axiom{}.

\begin{theorem}[RH from the first cause]\label{thm:riemannHypothesis_from_firstCause}
\yellow{}\ From the single extended decree, the Riemann hypothesis follows.
\leantag{riemannHypothesis\_from\_firstCause}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} Apply \cref{thm:riemannHypothesis_of_manifestation_and_boundary} to the absence of
engines, the first-cause projection, and the manifestation field. The machine axiom list reads
$[\code{propext},\ \code{Classical.choice},\ \code{Quot.sound},\ \axiom{}]$: the last word betrays
the taint. This is \emph{not} a proof of RH --- it is a reduction closed by decree, exactly as with
the twins.

The obvious suspicion --- that we merely renamed RH inside the law --- is answered by machine, and
the answer is subtle.

\begin{theorem}[the law asserts RH]\label{thm:riemannManifestation_asserts_RH}
\yellow{}\ Under the accepted twin boundary, the manifestation law is equivalent to RH.
\leantag{riemannManifestation\_asserts\_RH}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} The decree is no weaker than the conclusion, and we prove this rather than hide
it. But it is not a tautology: unlike a condemned bridge for which ``bridge $\iff$ RH'' holds
\emph{alone, greenly, with no hypotheses} (a verbatim renaming of the goal), the implication ``law
$\Rightarrow$ RH'' does not assemble at all without the boundary. What we decree is a causal
condition on top of a boundary already accepted for the twins, not a renamed goal --- one
architecture applied twice.

Finally, the decree admits no internal grounding. A bundle carrying the law itself together with a
witness that it came from beyond one's own horizon self-destructs.

\begin{theorem}[no internal grounding of the law]\label{thm:no_internalisedRiemannGround}
\green{}\ Internal self-grounding of the manifestation law is impossible; hence the Riemann cause is
unknowable from inside. \leantag{no\_internalisedRiemannGround}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof idea.} The bundle is formal --- ``beyond one's own horizon'' is simply the negation of
``grounded'', a tautological $P\wedge\neg P$, and we do not hide this. What pays for the form is a
genuine construction: the only internal trace of a deviation is a perpetual engine, gated by the
resolving scale --- itself the open twin node. This is not G\"odel and not a solution of RH: only the
pigeonhole wall, model-internal. The engine that would decide for us is forbidden by the same machine
with which we prove everything; the external door of the decree remains the only one.

\medskip

\noindent\emph{Honest status.} RH is \red{} open. Proven \green{} are the conditional theorems
(\cref{thm:riemannHypothesis_of_engine_bridge,thm:riemannHypothesis_of_two_transport,thm:riemann_of_liouville_bound}),
the rank/sign identities
(\cref{thm:liouville_eq_neg_one_pow_rank,thm:liouville_flip_of_mul_prime}), the closed input
\cref{thm:trivialBelowZeroClassification}, the circularity and manifestation theorems, and the
epistemic wall. \red{} inputs: the engine bridge (equivalently the two-transport bridge), the
Liouville bound, the Liouville--RH bridge, and a load-bearing spectral anchor (the Hilbert--P\'olya
operator itself, absent from \code{mathlib}). \yellow{} is only the route through the first cause,
whose price is disclosed by \cref{thm:riemannManifestation_asserts_RH}. Nowhere does a reduction
substitute for a proof~\cite{clay-rh}.
